About

Once upon a time there was a computer manufaturer called Commodore.

They started out by making typewriters and pocket calculators, but got their biggest sucesses in the VIC-20, Commodore 64, and the Amiga-line of personal computers.

The VIC-20 and Commodore 64 (the best selling computer brand of all times!) are no doubt "just" gaming systems of the early 1980's.'

The Amiga is something else.

It can certainly be argued that it was often used as a gaming machine, and it was certainly great in that. Many classic games originated on that platform, such as Lemmings as just one example.

But it was also a lot more than that. For example, the Scala suite of software commonly used in TV and display-sets would not exist if it wasn't for the Amiga.

And of course: for the U.S. market, the so-called Video Toaster was just an Amiga in disguise.

But most importantly IMO, it showed that a computer does not have to be a Windows or Mac PC. It can be something else, and still be viable.

And of course: it was a fun system to use!
